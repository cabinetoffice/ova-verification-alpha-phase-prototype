What you'll need

We need to ask you some details about yourself and the information you are requesting. This is to ensure that only veterans or people who have permission to access their records use this service.

Verifying your identity

As part of this service you will need to verify your identity.

You can do this by:

  • using the online Gov.uk Digital Identity service
  • applying by post (opens in a new tab)
  • going to the Post Office and using the in-branch verification service

Gov.uk Digital Identity is a service which allows you to create a secure online account to store your data.

Once you have one set up you won't need to input your personal information again in order to access online government services.

You can track and save your progress and come back to complete the process in multiple sessions if you need to. You will be asked to provide some of the following:

  • your passport
  • your driving licence
  • addresses you have lived at for the last 5 years and the dates you lived at them
  • your service number/li>
  • a passport style photo

Supporting someone else to apply for a veteran card

To support someone else to apply for a veteran card, you need to provide proof that:

  • you have their permission to apply on their behalf
  • you have the authority to verify their status

We'll ask you to upload either:

  • the person's written consent from you to access their information
  • a court order (for example, power of attorney) permitting the release of the information requested
  • evidence that you work for one of the approved services that are authorised to verify a veteran's status

If you cannot provide any of these you'll need to apply by post instead (opens in a new tab)

Verifying your own veteran status

If you've served in the armed forces, we'll ask you for the following details to help us find your information:

  • your service number
  • your enlistment and discharge dates
